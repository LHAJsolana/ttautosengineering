# How to Read a Used-Car Service Invoice

A service invoice can tell you more than a seller does.

But you need to read it carefully. A line that says "service done" is less useful than itemized parts, fluids, dates, mileage, and technician notes.

## What matters most

Look for:

- Date and mileage
- Exact oil specification
- Replaced parts, not just inspected parts
- Labor description
- Fault-code notes
- Recommendations declined by the owner
- Repeated repairs for the same symptom

The best invoices show a pattern of preventive maintenance, not only emergency repairs.

## Red flags

Be cautious with:

- Missing mileage
- Vague descriptions
- Cheap unknown fluids
- Repeated coolant top-ups with no repair
- Diagnostic charges with no listed fix
- "Customer declined" notes on important items

## Practical rule

An invoice is strongest when it proves what changed on the car. If it only proves the car visited a workshop, keep asking questions.
