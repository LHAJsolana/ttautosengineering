# BMW N47 vs B47: Which Used Diesel Is the Safer Buy?

The short answer is that the B47 is normally the safer default. It improved the main buying worry around the older N47, but it is not risk-free. A neglected B47 can still be a poor purchase, and a documented N47 can still make sense if the price reflects its inspection risk.

Use the full [BMW N47 vs B47 comparison](/compare/bmw-n47-vs-b47) when you want the side-by-side scoring.

## Why the N47 needs more caution

The N47 is known for timing-chain concern, especially because access can be expensive. That does not mean every N47 is bad. It means you should not buy one casually.

Before buying, look for:

- Timing-chain invoices or credible specialist inspection
- Frequent oil-service evidence
- A true cold start with no metallic chain noise
- Clean DPF, EGR, injector, and glow-system scan data
- No smoke, rough idle, or boost hesitation

<Callout type="warn" title="Do not buy on verbal reassurance">
If the seller says "they all sound like that" or refuses a cold start, treat the car as high risk until proven otherwise.
</Callout>

## Why the B47 is usually better

The B47 is generally the more mature diesel. It still needs proper oil service, cooling checks, and emissions-system verification, but the buying risk is usually easier to manage than an unknown N47.

Check the [BMW B47 guide](/powertrains/bmw-b47) and pay attention to EGR campaign history, DPF loading, coolant level, and stored emissions faults.

## The simple used-buy rule

Choose the B47 when the service history and scan data are clean. Choose the N47 only when timing evidence is strong and the price leaves room for risk. If both cars are vague, buy neither.
