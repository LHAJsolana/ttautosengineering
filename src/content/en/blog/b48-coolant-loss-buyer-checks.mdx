# BMW B48 Coolant Loss Checks Before You Buy

The BMW B48 can be a strong used petrol engine, but coolant loss changes the buying conversation quickly. A seller may describe a top-up as normal, yet modern sealed cooling systems should not need regular attention. Before buying, judge the whole system rather than only the expansion tank level.

## Inspect the evidence trail

Look around the thermostat housing, electric water pump area, radiator end tanks, hose junctions, and the underside after a proper warm test drive. Pink, white, or crusty residue matters even when the coolant level looks correct on viewing day.

## Watch temperature behavior

Use live data to compare requested and actual coolant temperature. Slow warm-up, temperature swings, fan noise after gentle driving, or heater inconsistency can point to thermostat control, sensor plausibility, or trapped air after a previous repair.

## Ask for invoices, not reassurance

A recent coolant top-up without pressure-test proof is not maintenance history. Ask whether the cooling system was pressure-tested, vacuum-filled, and checked again after heat cycles.

## Buying rule

A clean B48 with dry cooling hardware is a sensible used buy. A B48 with unexplained coolant loss should be priced for diagnosis before deposit, because overheating risk can turn a small leak into a major repair.
