# BMW B47 DPF and EGR Buying Signals

The BMW B47 is generally a more mature diesel than the earlier N47, but that does not make every used example low risk. Its condition depends heavily on oil-service discipline, driving pattern, cooling stability, and the health of the emissions system.

Start with the full [BMW B47 reliability and buying guide](/powertrains/bmw-b47), then use the checks below on the actual car.

## Read the driving pattern before the fault memory

A diesel used mainly for repeated short trips may struggle to complete DPF regeneration. Ask where the car was driven, how long its normal journeys were, and whether the seller noticed frequent cooling fans, increased idle speed, fuel-economy changes, or interrupted regenerations.

The odometer alone cannot tell you whether the DPF has lived an easy life. A higher-mileage motorway car can have a healthier emissions system than a low-mileage urban example.

## Demand useful scan data

A BMW-capable diagnostic scan should examine:

- Calculated soot and ash loading
- Distance and time since the last regeneration
- Exhaust-temperature sensor plausibility
- Differential-pressure readings
- EGR command and measured response
- Coolant-temperature behavior
- Stored and shadow fault codes

Our [DPF regeneration versus blockage guide](/insights/dpf-regeneration-vs-blockage) explains why a forced regeneration is not a repair when another fault prevents normal regeneration.

<Callout type="warn" title="Cleared codes are not clean evidence">
Recently erased faults remove context. Check readiness information, regeneration history, battery events, and whether the monitors have completed since the last reset.
</Callout>

## Verify EGR and cooling history

Check whether applicable EGR cooler campaigns were completed and inspect for unexplained coolant loss, dried residue, warning messages, or previous cooling-system work. A coolant problem must be diagnosed rather than dismissed as normal consumption.

The engine should reach and hold a sensible operating temperature. A thermostat fault can increase fuel use and make successful DPF regeneration more difficult.

## Test the complete car

From a true cold start, check cranking speed, idle quality, smoke, chain noise, injector balance, and warning messages. During the drive, look for smooth torque delivery without hesitation, limp mode, temperature instability, or transmission behavior that masks an engine problem.

For a common application, pair this inspection with the [BMW 3 Series used buying guide](/models/bmw-3-series) and use the [pre-purchase checklist](/buying-checklist) to keep the viewing consistent.

## Buyer verdict

A B47 with credible oil history, stable cooling, completed EGR work, healthy DPF data, and clean road behavior can be a strong long-distance choice. Walk away when the seller offers only cleared codes, vague motorway claims, or a warning light that supposedly needs "just a sensor."
