# EA888 Timing Chain Risk: What Buyers Should Understand

The EA888 family is not one single risk profile. Some versions are far better than others, but buyers still need to understand timing chain symptoms, oil service quality, and evidence of previous repair.

## Why the chain matters

A timing chain is designed to synchronize crankshaft and camshaft movement. When the chain stretches, tensioner control weakens, or guide wear increases, cam timing can drift.

That can create:

- Rattle on cold start
- Rough idle
- Cam/crank correlation faults
- Poor performance
- Expensive repair exposure if ignored

## What to check before buying

Ask for service records first. Long oil intervals and poor oil quality increase risk because chain tensioners and variable valve timing systems depend on clean oil pressure.

During inspection, listen on a cold start. A brief noise is not always a failure, but persistent metallic rattle deserves diagnosis before purchase.

## Practical buyer rule

If timing chain work has already been done properly, ask for the invoice and parts list. If it has not been done and the engine/era is known for risk, price the car as if you may own that job soon.
