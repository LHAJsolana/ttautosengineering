# Audi Q5 Quattro Inspection Checks Before Buying

Quattro branding does not remove maintenance risk. On a used Audi Q5, the exact generation, gearbox, tire condition, and service history decide whether the drivetrain is a strength or an expensive unknown.

## Confirm the actual hardware

Do not rely on the badge alone. Confirm the engine code, gearbox type, and quattro system fitted to the exact car. Service needs differ, and the wrong assumption can make a buyer miss fluid or adaptation issues.

## Check tires and vibration

Premium AWD cars dislike mismatched tires. Inspect brand, size, load rating, tread depth, wear pattern, and date codes. During the test drive, include tight turns, steady cruising, and gentle acceleration to feel for binding, shudder, or driveline vibration.

## Scan beyond the engine

Read gearbox, ABS, AWD, body, parking, and driver-assistance modules. Engine-only scans miss the issues that often make a Q5 expensive after purchase.

## Buying rule

A Q5 with boring invoices, matching tires, smooth shifts, and clean modules can be a strong family buy. Walk carefully when the seller cannot prove drivetrain service or explains vibration as normal quattro behavior.
