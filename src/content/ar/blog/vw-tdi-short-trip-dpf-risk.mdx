# VW TDI Short-Trip DPF Risk Before You Buy

A Volkswagen TDI that only drives short urban trips can look clean, start well, and still hide DPF trouble. Diesel particulate filters need temperature, time, and correct engine operation. When those conditions are missing, soot load, oil dilution, and incomplete regeneration become buying risks.

## Look for the usage pattern

Ask about commute length, motorway use, and recent warning lights. Very low annual mileage is not automatically good for a diesel. Repeated short journeys can stop regeneration before it completes.

## Read the right data

Request calculated soot load, ash estimate, differential pressure, exhaust temperature sensor data, last regeneration distance, and EGR-related faults. A generic no-code scan tells only a small part of the story.

## Check the thermostat

A diesel that runs too cool may regenerate too often or fail readiness tests. Log coolant temperature from cold and compare it to expected operating temperature after a realistic drive.

## Buying rule

Buy the TDI that matches the driving profile. If the car has short-trip history, rising oil level, repeated fan activity, or unclear DPF data, price in diagnosis before trusting the bargain.
